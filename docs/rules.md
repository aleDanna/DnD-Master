# D&D Basic Rules Summary

**Version 1.0 - Released November 2018**

This document contains a comprehensive summary of all rules from the D&D Basic Rules.

---

## Chapter 1: Step-by-Step Characters

- **Character Creation Process**
  - Choose a race (provides traits, abilities, sometimes languages)
  - Choose a class (defines character's capabilities)
  - Determine ability scores (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma)
  - Describe your character (personality, background, appearance)
  - Choose equipment (based on class and background)

- **Ability Scores**
  - Six abilities define a character: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
  - Scores range from 3 to 18 (typically), maximum 20 for player characters
  - Modifiers calculated as: (Score - 10) / 2, rounded down
  - **Generation Methods**
    - Standard array: 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8
    - Rolling: 4d6, drop lowest, six times
    - Point buy: Start at 8, spend 27 points (costs vary by score)
  - **Ability Score Summary**
    - Strength: Physical power, melee attacks, carrying capacity
    - Dexterity: Agility, reflexes, ranged attacks, AC, initiative
    - Constitution: Health, stamina, hit points, concentration
    - Intelligence: Learning, memory, reasoning, arcane magic
    - Wisdom: Awareness, intuition, insight, divine magic
    - Charisma: Personality, presence, force of will, social skills

- **Hit Points and Hit Dice**
  - Starting HP = Hit Die maximum + Constitution modifier
  - Hit Dice determined by class
  - Used for healing during short rests

- **Proficiency Bonus**
  - Starts at +2 at level 1
  - Increases based on character level
  - Applied to attack rolls, saving throws, and ability checks where proficient

## Chapter 2: Races

### Dwarf
- **Ability Score Increase**: Constitution +2
- **Age**: Mature around 50, live to 350 years
- **Size**: Medium (4-5 feet tall)
- **Speed**: 25 feet (not reduced by heavy armor)
- **Darkvision**: 60 feet
- **Dwarven Resilience**: Advantage on saves vs. poison, resistance to poison damage
- **Dwarven Combat Training**: Proficiency with battleaxe, handaxe, light hammer, warhammer
- **Tool Proficiency**: Smith's tools, brewer's supplies, or mason's tools
- **Stonecunning**: Double proficiency bonus on History checks related to stonework
- **Languages**: Common, Dwarvish

- **Subraces**
  - **Hill Dwarf**: Wisdom +1, HP increases by 1 per level
  - **Mountain Dwarf**: Strength +2, proficiency with light and medium armor

### Elf
- **Ability Score Increase**: Dexterity +2
- **Age**: Mature around 100, live to 750 years
- **Size**: Medium (5-6 feet tall)
- **Speed**: 30 feet
- **Darkvision**: 60 feet
- **Keen Senses**: Proficiency in Perception
- **Fey Ancestry**: Advantage on saves vs. charm, immune to magical sleep
- **Trance**: 4 hours of meditation instead of 8 hours sleep
- **Languages**: Common, Elvish

- **Subraces**
  - **High Elf**: Intelligence +1, one wizard cantrip, one extra language
  - **Wood Elf**: Wisdom +1, speed 35 feet, Mask of the Wild (hide in natural phenomena)

### Halfling
- **Ability Score Increase**: Dexterity +2
- **Age**: Mature around 20, live to 150 years
- **Size**: Small (about 3 feet tall)
- **Speed**: 25 feet
- **Lucky**: Reroll 1s on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws
- **Brave**: Advantage on saves vs. being frightened
- **Halfling Nimbleness**: Move through space of larger creatures
- **Languages**: Common, Halfling

- **Subraces**
  - **Lightfoot**: Charisma +1, can hide behind creatures one size larger
  - **Stout**: Constitution +1, advantage on saves vs. poison, resistance to poison damage

### Human
- **Ability Score Increase**: All ability scores +1
- **Age**: Mature in late teens, live less than a century
- **Size**: Medium (5-6 feet tall)
- **Speed**: 30 feet
- **Languages**: Common, one extra language of choice

## Chapter 3: Classes

### Cleric
- **Hit Die**: d8
- **Primary Ability**: Wisdom
- **Saving Throw Proficiencies**: Wisdom, Charisma
- **Armor Proficiencies**: Light armor, medium armor, shields
- **Weapon Proficiencies**: Simple weapons
- **Spellcasting**: Divine spellcaster, prepares spells from cleric list
- **Divine Domain**: Choose at 1st level (Life domain in Basic Rules)
  - Life Domain features: bonus proficiency (heavy armor), Disciple of Life (bonus healing)
- **Channel Divinity**: Special abilities from domain (2nd level)
  - Turn Undead: Force undead to flee
  - Preserve Life (Life Domain): Restore hit points
- **Destroy Undead**: Instantly destroy low-CR undead (5th level+)
- **Divine Intervention**: Call on deity for aid (10th level)

### Fighter
- **Hit Die**: d10
- **Primary Ability**: Strength or Dexterity
- **Saving Throw Proficiencies**: Strength, Constitution
- **Armor Proficiencies**: All armor, shields
- **Weapon Proficiencies**: Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Fighting Style**: Choose one at 1st level
  - Archery: +2 to ranged attack rolls
  - Defense: +1 AC while wearing armor
  - Dueling: +2 damage with one-handed weapon
  - Great Weapon Fighting: Reroll 1s and 2s on damage with two-handed weapons
  - Protection: Impose disadvantage on attacks against adjacent allies
  - Two-Weapon Fighting: Add ability modifier to off-hand damage
- **Second Wind**: Regain HP as bonus action (1d10 + fighter level)
- **Action Surge**: Take extra action (2nd level)
- **Martial Archetype**: Choose at 3rd level (Champion in Basic Rules)
  - Champion: Improved Critical (crit on 19-20), Remarkable Athlete, Additional Fighting Style
- **Extra Attack**: Attack twice per Attack action (5th level), three times (11th), four times (20th)
- **Indomitable**: Reroll failed saving throw (9th level)

### Rogue
- **Hit Die**: d8
- **Primary Ability**: Dexterity
- **Saving Throw Proficiencies**: Dexterity, Intelligence
- **Armor Proficiencies**: Light armor
- **Weapon Proficiencies**: Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords
- **Skill Proficiencies**: Four from rogue list
- **Expertise**: Double proficiency bonus on two skills (1st level), two more (6th level)
- **Sneak Attack**: Extra damage when advantage or ally adjacent to target
  - Starts at 1d6, increases every odd level
- **Thieves' Cant**: Secret language of rogues
- **Cunning Action**: Dash, Disengage, or Hide as bonus action (2nd level)
- **Roguish Archetype**: Choose at 3rd level (Thief in Basic Rules)
  - Thief: Fast Hands, Second-Story Work, Supreme Sneak, Use Magic Device, Thief's Reflexes
- **Uncanny Dodge**: Halve damage from seen attacker (5th level)
- **Evasion**: No damage on successful Dex save, half on fail (7th level)
- **Reliable Talent**: Minimum 10 on proficient ability checks (11th level)
- **Blindsense**: Detect hidden creatures within 10 feet (14th level)
- **Slippery Mind**: Proficiency in Wisdom saves (15th level)
- **Elusive**: No advantage against you unless incapacitated (18th level)
- **Stroke of Luck**: Turn miss into hit or treat failed check as 20 (20th level)

### Wizard
- **Hit Die**: d6
- **Primary Ability**: Intelligence
- **Saving Throw Proficiencies**: Intelligence, Wisdom
- **Armor Proficiencies**: None
- **Weapon Proficiencies**: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
- **Spellcasting**: Arcane spellcaster, learns spells in spellbook
  - Start with 3 cantrips and 6 1st-level spells in spellbook
  - Learn 2 new spells per level
  - Prepare spells equal to Intelligence modifier + wizard level
- **Spellbook Rules**
  - Copying a spell: 2 hours and 50 gp per spell level
  - Copying from your own spellbook: 1 hour and 10 gp per spell level
  - Can cast ritual spells from spellbook without preparing them
- **Arcane Recovery**: Recover spell slots during short rest (1st level)
  - Recover slots with combined level up to half wizard level (rounded up)
  - No slots of 6th level or higher
- **Arcane Tradition**: Choose at 2nd level (Evocation in Basic Rules)
  - Evocation Savant: Half time and cost to copy evocation spells
  - Sculpt Spells: Protect allies from your evocation spells (2nd level)
  - Potent Cantrip: Creatures take half damage on successful save (6th level)
  - Empowered Evocation: Add Int modifier to evocation damage (10th level)
  - Overchannel: Deal max damage with 1st-5th level spells (14th level)
- **Spell Mastery**: Cast 1st and 2nd level spells at will (18th level)
- **Signature Spells**: Two 3rd-level spells always prepared, cast once free (20th level)

## Chapter 4: Personality and Background

- **Character Details**
  - Name, physical description, alignment
  - Personality traits, ideals, bonds, flaws

- **Alignment**
  - Two components: morality (good, neutral, evil) and attitude toward society (lawful, neutral, chaotic)
  - Nine alignments: LG, NG, CG, LN, N, CN, LE, NE, CE

- **Backgrounds** (provide skills, tools, languages, equipment, and features)
  - **Acolyte**: Insight, Religion, 2 languages, Shelter of the Faithful

- **Inspiration**
  - Awarded by DM for good roleplaying
  - Spend to gain advantage on one roll
  - Can give to another player

## Chapter 5: Equipment

### Currency
- Copper pieces (cp), Silver pieces (sp), Electrum pieces (ep), Gold pieces (gp), Platinum pieces (pp)
- Exchange: 10 cp = 1 sp, 10 sp = 1 gp, 10 gp = 1 pp

### Armor
- **Light Armor**: Padded, Leather, Studded Leather
  - Add full Dex modifier to AC
- **Medium Armor**: Hide, Chain Shirt, Scale Mail, Breastplate, Half Plate
  - Add Dex modifier (max +2) to AC
- **Heavy Armor**: Ring Mail, Chain Mail, Splint, Plate
  - No Dex modifier, may have Strength requirement
  - Disadvantage on Stealth checks
- **Shields**: +2 AC

### Weapons
- **Simple Weapons**: Club, Dagger, Greatclub, Handaxe, Javelin, Light Hammer, Mace, Quarterstaff, Sickle, Spear, Light Crossbow, Dart, Shortbow, Sling
- **Martial Weapons**: Battleaxe, Flail, Glaive, Greataxe, Greatsword, Halberd, Lance, Longsword, Maul, Morningstar, Pike, Rapier, Scimitar, Shortsword, Trident, War Pick, Warhammer, Whip, Blowgun, Hand Crossbow, Heavy Crossbow, Longbow, Net

- **Weapon Properties**
  - Ammunition: Requires ammo, can recover half after battle
  - Finesse: Use Strength or Dexterity
  - Heavy: Small creatures have disadvantage
  - Light: Good for two-weapon fighting
  - Loading: One attack per action regardless of Extra Attack
  - Range: Two numbers (normal/long range)
  - Reach: Adds 5 feet to reach
  - Thrown: Can be thrown for ranged attack
  - Two-Handed: Requires two hands
  - Versatile: Can use one or two hands (different damage)

### Adventuring Gear
- Includes: Backpack, Bedroll, Rope, Torches, Rations, Waterskin, etc.
- **Equipment Packs**: Burglar's, Dungeoneer's, Explorer's, Priest's, Scholar's

### Tools
- Artisan's tools, Gaming sets, Musical instruments, Thieves' tools, etc.

### Mounts and Vehicles
- Mounts: Camel, Donkey, Elephant, Horse (draft, riding, warhorse), Mastiff, Pony
- Vehicles: Carriage, Cart, Chariot, Sled, Wagon, Ships

### Expenses
- **Lifestyle Expenses (per day)**
  - Wretched: Free (homeless, exposed to elements)
  - Squalid: 1 sp (leaky shack, minimal food)
  - Poor: 2 sp (simple lodging, basic food)
  - Modest: 1 gp (clean room, adequate food)
  - Comfortable: 2 gp (good food, private room)
  - Wealthy: 4 gp (fine accommodations, servants)
  - Aristocratic: 10+ gp (luxurious estate living)

### Food, Drink, and Lodging
- Ale (gallon): 2 sp
- Bread (loaf): 2 cp
- Cheese (hunk): 1 sp
- Inn stay (per day): Squalid 7 cp, Poor 1 sp, Modest 5 sp, Comfortable 8 sp, Wealthy 2 gp, Aristocratic 4 gp
- Meals (per day): Squalid 3 cp, Poor 6 cp, Modest 3 sp, Comfortable 5 sp, Wealthy 8 sp, Aristocratic 2 gp
- Rations (1 day): 5 sp
- Wine (common pitcher): 2 sp

## Chapter 6: Customization Options

### Multiclassing
- **Prerequisites**: Must meet ability score requirements for both classes
- **Proficiencies**: Limited proficiencies from new class
- **Spell Slots**: Combined based on spellcaster levels
- **Channel Divinity**: Don't gain extra uses from multiple classes

### Feats (Optional Rule)
- Take instead of Ability Score Improvement
- Examples in Player's Handbook

## Chapter 7: Using Ability Scores

### Ability Checks
- Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
- **Difficulty Class (DC)**: Very Easy (5), Easy (10), Medium (15), Hard (20), Very Hard (25), Nearly Impossible (30)

### Skills
- **Strength**: Athletics
- **Dexterity**: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **Intelligence**: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion
- **Wisdom**: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival
- **Charisma**: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion

### Passive Checks
- 10 + all modifiers (used for passive Perception, etc.)
- Advantage gives +5, disadvantage gives -5

### Working Together
- Help action gives advantage
- Group checks: succeed if half or more succeed

### Saving Throws
- d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
- Used to resist spells, traps, poisons, etc.

### Advantage and Disadvantage
- **Advantage**: Roll 2d20, take higher
- **Disadvantage**: Roll 2d20, take lower
- Multiple sources don't stack; advantage and disadvantage cancel out

## Chapter 8: Adventuring

### Time
- **Round**: 6 seconds (combat)
- **Minute**: 10 rounds
- **Hour, Day**: Exploration and downtime

### Movement
- **Travel Pace**
  - Fast: 400 feet/minute, 4 miles/hour, 30 miles/day (-5 passive Perception)
  - Normal: 300 feet/minute, 3 miles/hour, 24 miles/day
  - Slow: 200 feet/minute, 2 miles/hour, 18 miles/day (can stealth)
- **Difficult Terrain**: Costs 2 feet per foot moved
- **Climbing, Swimming, Crawling**: Costs extra movement (or full movement in difficult terrain)
- **Jumping**
  - Long jump: feet equal to Strength score (half from standing)
  - High jump: 3 + Strength modifier feet (half from standing)

### Environment
- **Falling**: 1d6 bludgeoning per 10 feet, max 20d6
- **Suffocating**: Can hold breath for 1 + Con modifier minutes (minimum 30 seconds)
- **Vision and Light**
  - Bright light: Normal vision
  - Dim light: Lightly obscured (disadvantage on Perception)
  - Darkness: Heavily obscured (effectively blinded)
- **Darkvision**: See in darkness as dim light (no color)
- **Blindsight**: Perceive without sight
- **Truesight**: See in magical darkness, invisibility, illusions, shapechangers, into Ethereal Plane

### Resting
- **Short Rest**: At least 1 hour
  - Can spend Hit Dice to heal
  - Some abilities recharge
- **Long Rest**: At least 8 hours (6 hours sleep minimum)
  - Regain all HP
  - Regain up to half total Hit Dice
  - Can't benefit from more than one per 24 hours

### Between Adventures
- Lifestyle expenses
- Downtime activities: Crafting, Practicing a Profession, Recuperating, Researching, Training

## Chapter 9: Combat

### Combat Sequence
1. Determine surprise
2. Establish positions
3. Roll initiative
4. Take turns in initiative order
5. Begin next round

### Initiative
- Dexterity check at combat start
- Determines turn order (highest first)
- Ties: DM decides for monsters, players decide among themselves

### Surprise
- Compare Stealth vs. passive Perception
- Surprised creatures can't move or act on first turn, no reactions until turn ends

### Your Turn
- **Move**: Up to your speed
- **Action**: One action (Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Ready, Search, Use an Object)
- **Bonus Action**: Only if feature/spell grants one
- **Free Interaction**: One object or environment interaction
- Can split movement before/after action

### Actions in Combat
- **Attack**: Make one melee or ranged attack (or more with Extra Attack)
- **Cast a Spell**: Cast a spell with casting time of 1 action
- **Dash**: Double movement for turn
- **Disengage**: Movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks
- **Dodge**: Attacks against you have disadvantage, advantage on Dex saves
- **Help**: Give ally advantage on next ability check or attack
- **Hide**: Make Stealth check to become hidden
- **Ready**: Prepare action for trigger (uses reaction)
- **Search**: Make Perception or Investigation check
- **Use an Object**: Interact with object requiring action

### Making an Attack
1. Choose target
2. Determine modifiers
3. Roll d20 + modifiers vs. AC
4. On hit, roll damage

- **Attack Rolls**: d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
- **Melee Attacks**: Usually Strength modifier
- **Ranged Attacks**: Usually Dexterity modifier
- **Finesse Weapons**: Choice of Strength or Dexterity

### Critical Hits and Misses
- **Natural 20**: Always hits, roll damage dice twice
- **Natural 1**: Always misses

### Damage and Healing
- **Damage Types**: Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder
- **Resistance**: Take half damage (round down)
- **Vulnerability**: Take double damage
- **Immunity**: Take no damage
- **Healing**
  - Can't exceed hit point maximum
  - Regain all HP on long rest
  - Spend Hit Dice during short rest (roll die + Con modifier per die)
  - Regain half total Hit Dice (minimum 1) on long rest
- **Temporary Hit Points**
  - Don't stack (use higher value)
  - Lost before regular HP
  - Not restored by healing
  - Disappear after long rest unless stated otherwise

### Dropping to 0 Hit Points
- **Instant Death**: If remaining damage equals or exceeds HP maximum
- **Falling Unconscious**: Drop to 0, fall unconscious
- **Death Saving Throws**: Roll d20 at start of each turn
  - 10+: Success
  - Below 10: Failure
  - 3 successes: Stabilize
  - 3 failures: Die
  - Natural 20: Regain 1 HP
  - Natural 1: 2 failures
- **Damage at 0 HP**: Causes death save failure (critical hit = 2 failures)
- **Stabilizing**: DC 10 Medicine check or healing

### Knocking a Creature Out
- Melee attack can knock unconscious instead of kill (attacker's choice)

### Cover
- **Half Cover**: +2 AC and Dex saves
- **Three-Quarters Cover**: +5 AC and Dex saves
- **Total Cover**: Can't be targeted directly

### Two-Weapon Fighting
- When attacking with light weapon, can use bonus action to attack with another light weapon
- Don't add ability modifier to damage of bonus attack (unless negative)

### Grappling
- Special melee attack
- Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics
- Success: Target is grappled (speed 0)
- Escape: Target's action, Athletics or Acrobatics vs. your Athletics

### Shoving
- Special melee attack
- Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics
- Success: Push 5 feet or knock prone

### Reactions
- One per round (resets at start of your turn)
- **Opportunity Attack**: When creature leaves your reach
  - One melee attack against the creature
  - Doesn't occur if creature uses Disengage or teleports

### Mounted Combat
- Mounting/dismounting costs half movement
- If mount moves involuntarily, DC 10 Dex save or fall prone
- Can force mount to Dash, Disengage, or Dodge
- Controlled vs. independent mounts

### Underwater Combat
- Melee weapons: Disadvantage unless dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident
- Ranged weapons: Auto-miss beyond normal range, disadvantage within normal range (unless crossbow, net, or thrown weapon like javelin)

## Chapter 10: Spellcasting

### Spell Basics
- **Spell Level**: 0 (cantrips) through 9th level
- **Cantrips**: Cast at will, don't use spell slots
- **Spell Slots**: Expended to cast spells, recovered on rest

### Casting a Spell
- **Casting Time**: Action, bonus action, reaction, minutes, or hours
- **Range**: Self, touch, or distance in feet
- **Components**
  - Verbal (V): Must speak
  - Somatic (S): Must gesture
  - Material (M): Requires specific items (can use component pouch or focus)
- **Duration**: Instantaneous, rounds, minutes, hours, or concentration
- **Targets**: Creature, object, point in space, self, area

### Concentration
- Some spells require concentration
- Lost if: cast another concentration spell, take damage (DC 10 or half damage, whichever is higher Con save), incapacitated, killed
- Can end voluntarily

### Areas of Effect
- **Cone**: Width equals length
- **Cube**: Size is length of each side
- **Cylinder**: Radius and height specified
- **Line**: Width is 5 feet unless stated
- **Sphere**: Radius from point of origin

### Saving Throws
- DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier
- Half damage on successful save for many damage spells

### Attack Rolls
- d20 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier

### Combining Magical Effects
- Effects with different names stack
- Same spell effects don't stack (use most potent)

### Ritual Casting
- Some spells can be cast as rituals
- Takes 10 extra minutes
- Doesn't expend spell slot

## Chapter 12: Monsters

### Monster Statistics
- **Armor Class (AC)**: Target number to hit
- **Hit Points (HP)**: Damage before death
- **Speed**: Movement per round (walking, climbing, flying, swimming, burrowing)
- **Ability Scores**: Same six as characters
- **Saving Throws**: Bonuses to specific saves
- **Skills**: Proficiency in certain skills
- **Damage Vulnerabilities/Resistances/Immunities**: Special damage interactions
- **Condition Immunities**: Immune to certain conditions
- **Senses**: Darkvision, blindsight, tremorsense, truesight, passive Perception
- **Languages**: Languages understood and spoken
- **Challenge Rating (CR)**: Difficulty indicator and XP value

### Type of Creatures
- Aberrations, Beasts, Celestials, Constructs, Dragons, Elementals, Fey, Fiends, Giants, Humanoids, Monstrosities, Oozes, Plants, Undead

### Special Abilities
- Legendary Actions: Extra actions at end of other turns
- Lair Actions: Special actions in creature's lair
- Legendary Resistance: Automatically succeed on failed saves

### Monsters by Challenge Rating
- **CR 0**: Awakened Shrub, Baboon, Badger, Bat, Cat, Commoner, Crab, Deer, Eagle, Frog, Giant Fire Beetle, Goat, Hawk, Hyena, Jackal, Lizard, Octopus, Owl, Quipper, Rat, Raven, Scorpion, Sea Horse, Spider, Vulture, Weasel

## Chapter 13: Building Combat Encounters

### Encounter Difficulty
- **Easy**: Doesn't tax resources, victory guaranteed
- **Medium**: One or two scary moments, may need healing
- **Hard**: Could go badly, possible character death
- **Deadly**: Potentially lethal, requires good tactics

### XP Thresholds by Level
- Level 1: Easy 25, Medium 50, Hard 75, Deadly 100
- Level 5: Easy 250, Medium 500, Hard 750, Deadly 1,100
- Level 10: Easy 600, Medium 1,200, Hard 1,900, Deadly 2,800
- Level 20: Easy 2,800, Medium 5,700, Hard 8,500, Deadly 12,700

### Encounter Multipliers
- 1 monster: ×1
- 2 monsters: ×1.5
- 3-6 monsters: ×2
- 7-10 monsters: ×2.5
- 11-14 monsters: ×3
- 15+ monsters: ×4

### The Adventuring Day
- Parties can handle 6-8 medium/hard encounters per day
- Short rests typically needed at 1/3 and 2/3 through day

## Appendix A: Conditions

- **Blinded**: Can't see, auto-fail sight checks, attacks have disadvantage, attacks against have advantage
- **Charmed**: Can't attack charmer, charmer has advantage on social checks
- **Deafened**: Can't hear, auto-fail hearing checks
- **Frightened**: Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source visible, can't move closer to source
- **Grappled**: Speed 0, ends if grappler incapacitated or removed from reach
- **Incapacitated**: Can't take actions or reactions
- **Invisible**: Can't be seen, heavily obscured, attacks have advantage, attacks against have disadvantage
- **Paralyzed**: Incapacitated, can't move or speak, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage, hits from within 5 feet are crits
- **Petrified**: Turned to stone, incapacitated, unaware, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, resistance to all damage, immune to poison/disease
- **Poisoned**: Disadvantage on attacks and ability checks
- **Prone**: Can only crawl, disadvantage on attacks, attacks within 5 feet have advantage, attacks beyond 5 feet have disadvantage
- **Restrained**: Speed 0, attacks have advantage against, attack rolls have disadvantage, disadvantage on Dex saves
- **Stunned**: Incapacitated, can't move, speak falteringly, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage
- **Unconscious**: Incapacitated, can't move or speak, unaware, drops held items, falls prone, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage, hits from within 5 feet are crits

### Exhaustion Levels
1. Disadvantage on ability checks
2. Speed halved
3. Disadvantage on attacks and saves
4. HP maximum halved
5. Speed reduced to 0
6. Death
